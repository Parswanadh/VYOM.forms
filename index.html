About Us

We are a passionate community of astrophysics enthusiasts dedicated to exploring the cosmic mysteries that lie beyond our planet. From stellar evolution to black holes, from exoplanets to cosmology, we dive deep into the wonders of the universe through observations, research, and innovative projects.


Our club serves as a gateway to the cosmos, fostering an environment where curiosity about the universe meets cutting-edge astronomical research and discovery.

Deep Sky Observation Night

Join us for monthly stargazing sessions using our advanced telescopes to observe nebulae, galaxies, and star clusters. We'll explore the Orion Nebula, Andromeda Galaxy, and other celestial wonders while learning about their formation and evolution. Perfect for beginners and experienced astronomers alike.

Every 2nd Friday of the month

Exoplanet Detection Workshop

Hands-on workshop where students learn to detect exoplanets using the transit method. We'll analyze real data from the Kepler Space Telescope and learn about the techniques used to discover planets orbiting distant stars. Participants will process light curves and identify potential planetary candidates.

Quarterly workshop series

Astrophotography Competition

Annual competition showcasing the best astronomical photographs taken by club members. Categories include deep-sky objects, planetary imaging, and wide-field star trails. Winners receive recognition and prizes, with their work displayed in the physics department. Technical workshops on imaging techniques are provided.

Annual event - Spring semester

Black Hole Simulation Project

Collaborative research project using Python and specialized software to simulate black hole behavior and gravitational lensing effects. Students work in teams to model accretion disks, Hawking radiation, and spacetime curvature. Results are presented at the annual undergraduate research symposium.

Semester-long project

Stellar Evolution Study Group

Weekly study sessions focusing on stellar lifecycle, from stellar nurseries to supernovae and neutron stars. We analyze HR diagrams, study stellar spectra, and use computational models to understand how stars evolve. Guest lectures from astronomy professors and graduate students enhance the learning experience.

Weekly meetings - Wednesdays 7PM

Space Mission Analysis

In-depth analysis of current and upcoming space missions including James Webb Space Telescope, Artemis program, and Mars rovers. Students research mission objectives, technical specifications, and scientific discoveries. Regular presentations keep members updated on the latest space exploration developments.

Monthly mission briefings
